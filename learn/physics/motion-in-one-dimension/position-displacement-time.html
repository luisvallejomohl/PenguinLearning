Position, Displacement and Time

Introduction

There are 5 variables in 1D motion (that we teach about in PenguinLearning): Position, Displacement, Time, Velocity and Acceleration. Today you will read about position and displacement.

What is position?

Position is a measure of where things are located, hence its name. It is the distance between the object and an object, usually the ground, but it can be any object, even you maybe! Position is measured in meters(m), and its mathematical symbol is x. It is a vector, so if your position is [3 m] something and you are to the right of the ground, someone [6 m] to the left of you has a position of [-3 m].

What is displacement

Displacement is simply the change in position. Unlike position, it is not measured relative to something. The symbol is Δx, where Δ means/(is read as) Change in. Displacement is a vector, just like position

Time

Time measures how long it has been since time was 0. The unit for time is the second, s.The mathematical symbol for time is, not surprisingly, t. When t = [0 s] is totally arbitrary.
